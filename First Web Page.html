Cricket

The exact origins of cricket are unknown, but some experts believe it was invented in England during Saxon or Norman times. Some evidence suggests that cricket may have been played as early as the 13th century. The first recorded cricket match was in 1646 in Kent, England. The laws of cricket were established in 1744, and the world's first cricket club was formed in 1760. Cricket is a bat and ball sport played by two teams on an oval. It involves batting, fielding, and bowling. Cricket is most popular in former British colonies like India, Pakistan, South Africa, and Australia.


Father of Cricket

William Gilbert Grace is known as the father of cricket.

He played first-class cricket for a record-equalling 44 seasons, from 1865 to 1908, during which he captained England, Gloucestershire, the Gentlemen, Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC), the United South of England Eleven (USEE), and several other teams.
